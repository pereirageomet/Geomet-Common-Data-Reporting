## Problem statement
Inconsistent use of units, measurement formats, and reporting conventions across geometallurgical disciplines (e.g. comminution, flotation, leaching, mineralogy) presents a significant barrier to the integration, comparison, and downstream use of geometallurgical data. Laboratories, mining companies, and study teams often report equivalent measurements using different units or structures, reporting results in hard-to-consume data formats (e.g., PDFs), which leads to ambiguity, increased interpretation effort, and loss of data value.

Geometallurgical datasets are typically high-volume and multidisciplinary, combining geological context, spatial information, sample metadata, and laboratory test results. While other mining disciplines benefit from consistent unit standards and mature data systems, geometallurgy has historically evolved through laboratory-specific reporting practices.

The lack of standardised unit conventions reduces data interoperability, complicates database design, and impedes advanced analytics, reconciliation, and reuse of historical datasets. Addressing reporting consistency, especially for naming conventions across different data sets, and providing different testing types with an unified structure that eases reporting,  is therefore a foundational requirement for effective common data reporting in geometallurgy.


## Objective
This guideline aims at defining and aligning consistent units, measurement conventions, and reporting scheme across common geometallurgical sampling and testing contexts, enabling efficient data consumption in Geometallurgical programs.
