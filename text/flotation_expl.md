The reporting structure is divided into logical categories to capture the full "story" of a flotation test, from the initial sample properties to the final kinetic results. 

1. Metadata and Sample Context
  * Report Header & Metadata: Establishes the "who, when, and where," including laboratory details and test types. 
  * Sample Metadata: Covers the physical and geological properties of the head sample. It distinguishes between Measured properties (e.g., mass, moisture) and Reported properties (e.g., ore type, lithology) provided by the who requested the test. 

2. Test Parameters (The "Inputs")
Parameters are the controlled variables that give the test its context. 
  * Grinding Conditions: Details on the mill method, media composition, and the target P80​ (size distribution). 
  * Hydrodynamic & Physical Conditions: Factors such as impeller speed, airflow rates, gas types, and cell volumes. These are applied to all stages.
  * Stage-Specific Parameters: Reagent dosages as well as data recorded during specific cycles, including water chemistry (pH, Eh, dissolved oxygen). 

3. Outputs and Results (The "Findings")
The guidelines separate raw measurements from interpreted findings:
  * Stage Outputs: Data like concentrate pulp flowrates and dry masses for individual stages. 
  * Test Outputs: Overall outcomes such as final concentrate grades and tailings mass. 
  * Test Results: The high-level findings, including mass yield, metal/mineral recovery, and kinetic rate constants.
